ORGAN DONATION, LET'S TALK

“ What do you call a rash driver who doesn't wear a seatbelt? An Organ Donor.”
Caught your attention? Not yet? Maybe this will.

5,00,000 - The number of people that die every year waiting for a transplant. And the others? Wait for a match that can be as long as 18 years.
2,00,000 - The number of people that await kidney transplant. Only 1 in 10,000 manage to receive one.
50,000 - The number of people that await a heart transplant. But in 2017, only 339 donations were witnessed.
586 - The number of Airbus planes, that can be filled by the people awaiting an organ transplant.
8 - The number of lives one person can save.

But why should I be concerned?

India stands as a country with one of the lowest organ donation rates in the world, with 0.86 donors per million population. Not even an entire, 1 person per million. Alarming? It should be.

Now, let's talk facts

"You make Organ Donation sound so easy. If saving a life was that easy, Why are there such a shortage of donors then? "

That is because of all the registered Donors, very few die in a manner that allows for their organs to be harvested. Yes, only those declared brain dead are eligible to donate after death in India. This is called Deceased Donation.

1.5 lakh people die due to brain deaths caused because of accidents.
There is a need for 2,00,000 kidneys, 50,000 hearts and 50,000 livers every year approximately.
Even if 5 to 10% of all brain deaths are harvested properly, technically, there would never be a need for a living person to donate in India.

"But you just said only deceased people can donate, why would a living person need to donate? Do I need to donate my organs while I'm alive, if I sign up as a donor? "
Absolutely not. You do not have to donate your organs while you are alive, unless and until you specifically wish to do so.

Usually only kidney or liver transplants happen as far as living Donation is concerned, and only amongst close friends, distant relatives and immediate family. Distant relatives and close friends need to get proper authorization from a government panel before they can donate.

"Why is this the first time I'm hearing about all this?"
You can blame it on the general lack of awareness for this noble cause. The religious beliefs of many people have a part to play too. It doesn't help that movies have formed the belief that there is a black market for organ sale. That really isn't the case. Yes, we checked.
Does my family have to bear the expenses of my donation?
Absolutely not. Not one rupee would be charged on the donor's family. The cost of the entire process would be borne by those receiving the organs.
What if I pledge my organs and don't wish to donate?
In India, currently the situation is such that regardless of whether you pledge your organs or not, the ultimate decision lies with your family. Legally the next of kin, decide whether your organs get to be donated or not, even if you have pledged yourself. They get to decide regardless of whether you have opted for organ donation. Unless and until they provide written consent, and sign the required forms, your organs do not get donated.
Does my body get disfigured after donation? What if Doctors don't put in effort to save my life, knowing I carry a donor card?
No, your body does not get disfigured. Organs are removed through a single precision incision. It is treated with the same care and respect as was the case when you were alive. Yes, heroes get this treatment. Doctors are legally bound to do everything medically possible to save your lives. A panel of 4 medical experts need to declare you brain dead before your organs can even be considered for donation.
"How do I help you guys?"
Do what we're doing. Help us spread awareness. Speak up against those who spread myths.
"Spreading awareness is great. But can I pledge my organs?"
Absolutely. You can register yourself at www.giftanorgan.youngindians.net if you want to.
"What do I have to do if I pledge my organs?"
Firstly, the one most important thing you can do, is inform your family and close friends of this noble decision you have decided to take up. Discuss everything with them, be sure to include their considerations and then take the pledge.
You can sign up at the above link, and fill up the required details in the online form. You will then be sent a virtual registered donor card that you can print out and carry with you at all times.
"Is there anything I have to keep in mind?"
Keep in mind that the card you receive once you register is not a legally binding document or a legal entity. It merely expresses your wish to be an organ donor.
Remember to explain to your next of kin, the sentiment or motivation behind your reason to pledge yourself, so they may carry out your wishes in case the situation arises.
And if you're still hesitant to pledge, that's completely alright. Help us in spreading the message as far as you can. One person or a hundred. We do what we can, how we can.
